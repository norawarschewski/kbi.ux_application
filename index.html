KBI UX Incubator

Apply by March 20, 2026 for the KBI UX Incubator: Application Form

While thousands of languages and writing systems are used around the world, most digital products are still designed for only a small subset of them. As a result, many scripts and cultural contexts are poorly supported in apps, websites, and software interfaces. The Donald Knuth & Charles Bigelow UX Incubator seeks to address this gap by focusing on how User Experience (UX) design can better support diverse scripts, languages, and cultural ways of interacting with digital technology.

Quick Facts ➤ July/August 2026 ; 5 weeks ; weekly class time of approximately 5 hours ; regular attendance of synchronous/live sessions is expected

What participants can expect ➤ Work on a real-world UX challenge with a focus on script diversity and cultural context ⬤ Learn and apply UX methods through a structured, guided design sprint ⬤ Collaborate in small, international teams using problem-based approaches ⬤ Learn from leading experts through weekly guest lectures and workshops ⬤ Explore how writing systems, language, and culture shape digital experiences ⬤ Free tuition and access to all required applications ⬤ a Course Certificate following successful completion of the program

What you need to bring ➤ Curiosity ⬤ An interest in design, UX, type and digitally disadvantaged writing systems ⬤ Willingness to collaborate and actively contribute in group-based work ⬤ No advanced UX or Figma skills required ⬤ Commitment to attend sessions regularly and participate in discussions ⬤ Some time for independent and group work outside of workshops ⬤ Respect for cultural perspectives and sensitivity when working with languages and scripts beyond your own ⬤ Readiness to experiment, iterate, and learn through feedback

KBI’s mission is to provide equitable access to specialized training, mentorship, and resources that are often inaccessible to designers outside established industry or academic networks. By offering tuition-free participation, expert-led workshops, and software support, the incubator creates a space where designers from diverse backgrounds can enhance their UX knowledge and develop first solutions to real-world problems.
